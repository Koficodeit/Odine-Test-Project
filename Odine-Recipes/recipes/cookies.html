Cookies

Cookies are delightful, bite-sized treats enjoyed by people of all ages for their sweet taste and satisfying texture. Typically made from a dough of flour, sugar, and butter, cookies can be baked into a variety of shapes and sizes, from soft and chewy to crispy and crunchy. They can be flavored and enhanced with a myriad of ingredients such as chocolate chips, nuts, dried fruits, and spices, making them endlessly customizable. Perfect as a snack, dessert, or even a homemade gift, cookies are cherished for their comforting and nostalgic qualities, often evoking memories of home baking and festive celebrations.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Butter
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Baking Powder

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a bowl, cream together softened butter and sugar until smooth. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  3. Add Dry Ingredients: Combine flour, baking powder (or soda), and salt; gradually add to the creamed mixture. Stir in chocolate chips.
  4. Drop Dough: Drop by rounded spoonfuls onto ungreased cookie sheets.
  5. Bake: Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until edges are nicely browned.
  6. Cool and Serve: Allow cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to wire racks to cool completely.
