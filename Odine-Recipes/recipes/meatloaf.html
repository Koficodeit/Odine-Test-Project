Meatloaf

Meatloaf is a classic comfort food that combines ground meat, typically beef or a mixture of meats, with breadcrumbs, eggs, and a blend of seasonings. Often shaped into a loaf and baked, meatloaf is known for its hearty and satisfying nature. It is commonly glazed with a tangy tomato-based sauce or a sweet ketchup topping that caramelizes as it cooks, adding a flavorful crust. Versatile and easy to prepare, meatloaf can be customized with various ingredients like onions, garlic, herbs, and even vegetables. Served hot from the oven, it is a beloved family meal that pairs well with mashed potatoes, vegetables, or a fresh salad.

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef
  • Bread Crumbs
  • Eggs
  • Onion
  • Ketchup/Tomatoes Sauce

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine ground beef, bread crumbs, beaten eggs, chopped onion, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined.
  3. Shape Loaf: Shape the mixture into a loaf and place in a lightly greased loaf pan.
  4. Add Topping: Spread ketchup or tomato sauce over the top of the meatloaf.
  5. Bake: Bake for 1 hour, or until the meatloaf is no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear.
  6. Cool and Serve: Let the meatloaf rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
