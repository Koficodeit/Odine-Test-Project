Pancakes

Pancakes are a beloved breakfast staple, known for their fluffy texture and golden-brown exterior. Made from a simple batter of flour, eggs, milk, and baking powder, they are cooked on a griddle or frying pan until light and airy. Often served in stacks, pancakes can be enjoyed with a variety of toppings such as butter, syrup, fresh fruits, whipped cream, or nuts, making them endlessly customizable. Their comforting taste and versatility make pancakes a favorite morning treat, perfect for leisurely weekend breakfasts or special brunch gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Baking Powder
  • Sugar

steps

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together milk and eggs. Add melted butter and stir.
  3. Combine: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix.
  4. Cook Pancakes: Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake.
  5. Flip: Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges are set, then flip and cook until browned on the other side.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with butter, syrup, and your favorite toppings.
